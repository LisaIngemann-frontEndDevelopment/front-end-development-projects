Historical Launches Timeline

Scroll to see more of the timeline

First Photo

October 24.
The first photo of earth was taken from space by a German v2 rocket with a camera created by Clyde Holliday.

First Launch at Cape Canaveral

July 24.
Bumper 8 had the first launch at Cape Canaveral part of a region called Space Coast and it is the site of the Cape Canaveral Air Force Station.

Artificial Earth Satellite

First Animal In Space

October 4.
Sputnik-1 was the world’s first artificial Earth satellite and it was launched by The Soviet Union.

November 3.
Laika the dog became the first animal to orbit earth. She sat inside Sputnik-2.

First Human In Space

April 12.
Vostok 1 was the first spacecraft launched with a human aboard. The soviet astronaut Yuri Gagarin became the first human in space.

First Successful Planetary Encounter

August 27.
The Mariner 2, a space probe sent to Venus, was the first robotic space probe to have a successful planetary encounter. It measured the temperature distribution on the surface of Venus and basic measurements of its atmosphere.

First Woman In Space

June 16-19.
Vostok 6 was a spacecraft launched with astronaut Valentina Tereshkova aboard that became the first woman in space.

First Spacewalk

March 18-19.
Voskhod 2 was a soviet crewed space mission where Alexei Leonov, one of the 2 crewmembers aboard, conducted the first spacewalk for 12 minutes in a specialized spacesuit.

First Humans to Leave Earths Orbit

December 21-27.
Apollo 8 was the first crewed spacecraft to leave Earth’s orbit, orbit the moon and return. The 3 crew members were the first humans to witness and photograph an Earthrise.

First Moonlanding

July 16-24.
Apollo 11 was the first spacecraft that landed humans on the moon. They landed on July 20 and Neil Armstrong became the first human to step onto the moon’s surface July 21. They brought lunar samples back to Earth.

Failed Moonlanding

April 11-17.
Apollo 13 was the third crewed mission to land on the moon, but the lunar landing was aborted after an oxygen tank exploded. The crew looped around the moon and returned safely to earth.

First Contact With Jupiter

March 2.
Pioneer 10 is a space probe that was the first spacecraft to reach Jupiter.

Joint Mission Between US and USSR

July 17-19.
Apollo-Soyuz was the first joint mission between the US and USSR which involved the docking of an Apollo command and service module and the Soviet Soyuz 19 capsule.

First Successfull Landing Mission on Mars

July 20.
Viking 1 was the second spacecraft to soft-land on Mars and the first to successfully preform its landing mission. The first spacecraft to soft-land was the Soviet Union’s Mars 3, but it stopped transmitting after 14,5 seconds.

First Reusable Space vehicle

April 12-14.
The space shuttle Columbia was the first reusable space vehicle.

First Untethered Spacewalk

February 3-11.
The space shuttle Challenger's fourth flight included the first untethered spacewalk.

Challenger Disaster

January 28.
The space shuttle Challenger broke apart 73 seconds into its 10th mission killing all 7 crew members. It was the first orbiter destroyed in flight.

Hubble Space Telescope

April 24-29.
The 35th mission of the American Space Shuttle Program used the space shuttle Discovery to launch the Hubble Space Telescope into Earth’s orbit.

First Spacestation With Rover on Mars

December 4.
The Mars Pathfinder is an American robotic spacecraft that was the first to land a base station with a rover on Mars.

Zarya, First Piece of the ISS

November 20.
First piece of the International Space Station, Zarya, was launched aboard a Russian proton rocket.

Colombia Disaster

February 1.
Space Shuttle Columbia disintegrated during re-entry on the end of its 28th mission since its first mission in 1981 which resulted in the death of all 7 crew members.

First Exploration of Pluto

January 19.
New Horizons was launched in 2006 and was the first spacecraft to explore Pluto which it achieved on July 14, 2015.

Investigating Jupiter

August 5.
Juno is a NASA space probe orbiting the planet Jupiter. It does a scientific investigation of the planet including measuring the planet's composition, gravity field, magnetic field and polar magnetosphere.

Indias First Interplanetary Mission

November 5.
The Mars Orbiter Mission was India’s first interplanetary mission and is a space probe orbiting Mars and the least expensive Mars mission.

First Successful Vertical Landing

Nov 23.
New Shepard was the first suborbital booster rocket to make a successful vertical landing.

First Successful Rocket Landing on a Drone Ship

April 8.
A SpaceX Falcon 9 carried a dragon cargo capsule to the International Space Station and returned to perform the first successful rocket landing on a drone ship. This was the fifth attempt of recovering a spent Falcon 9 rocket with the four other failing because of a too fast landing or tipping over after touchdown.

First Successful Simultaneous Reusable Rocket Landing

February 6.
SpaceX Falcon Heavy’s maiden flight releasing an electric tesla sports car into space that will reach beyond the orbit of Mars. The falcon achieved the first simultaneous reusable rocket landing, but the center core stage was lost during a landing attempt at sea.

Test Flight of Crew Dragon

March 2.
A SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket launched a Crew Dragon spacecraft on an uncrewed test flight to the International Space Station. They are the first to work towards the goal of carrying astronauts to and from the International Space Station.

Amos-17 Mission

SpaceX is targeting Tuesday, August 6 for launch of AMOS-17 from Space Launch Complex 40 (SLC-40) at Cape Canaveral Air Force Station, Florida. The launch window opens at 6:53 p.m. EDT, or 22:53 UTC, and closes at 8:21 p.m. EDT, or 00:21 UTC on August 7. The satellite will be deployed approximately 31 minutes after liftoff.

Falcon 9’s first stage for the AMOS-17 mission previously supported the Telstar-19 VANTAGE mission in July 2018 and the Es’hail-2 mission in November 2018.

Juno Mission

Commanding Juno after years of testing was at first a nerve-racking experience for the scientists, engineers and countless other collaborators from across the world. The team worked as a family to make sure that launch was a success, that all nine on-board instruments operate as designed, that navigation, once in flight, goes smoothly, and that the mission-critical Juno Orbit Insertion went exactly as planned.

"We look at view graphs and charts and analyses all the time – what blows my mind is when I go out at night, and I look up at Jupiter, and I realize, we’re there." — Rick Nybakken, Project Manager 2012-2017, JPL

Team Members: Heidi Becker, Scott Bolton, Jack Connerney, Jennifer Delavan, Maria Schellpfeffer, Paul Steffes, Marla Thornton

Zarya Mission

Zarya is the first module of the International Space Station. Заря (Zarya) means “dawn” and was launched on 20 November 1998 by a Proton rocket from the Baikonur Cosmodrome in Kazakhstan. In December 1998, Space Shuttle Endeavour approached Zarya and docked the US module Unity.
